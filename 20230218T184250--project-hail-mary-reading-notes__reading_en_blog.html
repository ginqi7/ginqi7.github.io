Project Hail Mary Reading Notes

Author: Andy Weir Other works: The Martian

The second Andy Weir novel I read. His science fiction novels are still relatively easy to read, with no particularly advanced concepts. Build a crisis environment that uses basic physics concepts to solve problems step by step.

This time, the protagonist is not a hero who does not turn back to save the world; he is also timid. But he is still a man of integrity at heart, and when he can't choose, he still acts for the world's benefit.

In the end, it is more decisive to save friends than to save the world.

The final ending is quite interesting, avoiding the scene depicting the tribulated earth or the hero's return. Instead, it opens up a whole new world.

And the protagonist, until the end, is just an ordinary teacher.
